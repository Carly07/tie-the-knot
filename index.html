Inspiration

Your wedding theme and colour choice will set the tone for your whole day. Check out our top 10 wedding themes below...

Country Barn

Typically more relaxed, natural and full of local, homespun touches (like handmade wood tables and chairs in a barn venue), rustic weddings have been number one on this list for a few years now.

Beach

The smell of the salt air, the sound of the tide and the feel of fresh sea breeze on your cheeks as you exchange vows. Is there anything better than a wedding by the shore?

Garden Party

A wedding in the garden is romantic and natural, with lush beauty built right into your site. But if you can’t bring your wedding into the garden, you can always bring the garden to you, whether you’re celebrating indoors or out.

Enchanted Forest

This magical woodland wedding has the wow-factor thanks to whimslical lighting, trailing foliage, warm wooden details and a slightly-bohemian vibe.

Festival

Festivals are hugely popular events in the UK, celebrating music, food and culture. If you want a wedding that’s entertainment-focused, informal and all about fun, food and flower crowns then a festival-themed wedding is perfect for you.

Autumnal

Fall has gradually taken over as the most popular wedding season. For your autumn nuptials, embrace the chilly air and enjoy an excuse to wear a fun fur stole, not to mention eat mini apple hand-pies, cider doughnuts and pumpkin-spice cookies.

Winter Wonderland

Frosted window panes, deep color palettes and merry holiday spirits make more couples want to say “I do” in winter. So why not create a magical Winter wonderland full of sparkling, glimmering and bright winter decor.

Carnival

Coconut shies, fairground horses, bright colourways, festival lights and all the fun of the fair – we love that the carnival theme is back, bolder, brighter and with a smattering of classic vintage. So roll up, roll up for a wedding party like no other.

Vintage

Why not make your “something old” your wedding theme? Sprinkle antique touches and other heirloom details throughout your attire and décor for a timeless, vintage feel and for your last hoorah, finish off the vintage theme by driving away in a classic getaway car, like an old Porsche or Volkswagen.

Traditional

This grandoise typically involves a church ceremony with the bride wearing a classically styled, usually princess ballgown-style wedding dress, followed by formal celebrations. You'll want to pull out all the stops and include a exquisite sit-down three-course dinner.
